Disgrace

You hunt rogue entities to protect humanity because they were not always dangerous. At first they were harmless remainders, small pieces of reality that did not fully disappear, repeating quiet, meaningless actions without awareness. Then something changed. Some began to move, react, and learn, breaking their patterns and becoming unpredictable. The Red Box can force them back into a stable state, but it does not cure them. It only delays what they are becoming, and every successful capture teaches the others how to adapt faster.
`E` captures when an entity is in sight and within range. Each capture grants a power, raises the pressure in the maze, and makes the surviving entities feel more aware of what is being done to them.
`F` stabilizes the Red Box but slows you and burns stamina. `C` lets you slide out of a bad line if you have momentum. The box does not heal rogue entities. It only forces them back into temporary stability while it struggles to contain things that no longer follow rules.
If the box fails, escapes return worse than before. You are not just hunting entities. You are trying to stop something spreading, something learning, before there is nothing left to contain and nothing left to protect.
